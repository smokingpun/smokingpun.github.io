How Content Strategy Can Help Your Business

You know that great content will help your users understand your product and get excited about it, as well as bumping you up the search rankings. Great! But it can be hard keeping your content under control. As for optimising the content that you have: well, you don't really have time. And what should you look for? This is where I come in. My name is Hannah Adcock and I'm a content strategist. I can help you:

Control content so it is user-focused, meaningful and future-friendly

Save time because you'll only produce content that is necessary

Make money because curated content will turn site visitors into customers

How can I do this? Read on:

.

Analysis and Auditing

Your problem: some of our content isn't that great: we're not sure though what to keep and what to bin

I'll analyse your content – your website, social media and any publications – to see whether it's supporting your key business objectives and is user-friendly. I'll also do a content audit of your existing web material – or part of it at least. A content audit usually involves listing what content is on your site, where it is located and whether it is doing a good job.

Analytics

Your problem: we're not turning enough site visitors into customers: why not?

First, we need to see how people are engaging with your site. To do this, I'll use Google Analytics, KISSmetrics, Crazy Egg, Topsy – basically, whatever's most relevant and fits your budget. Are site visitors making it through your sales funnel? Is your 'buy now' button in a place that only 10% of your users ever venture? I'll then have a think about why your users are behaving as they are. We could also do some user-testing at this point. What do your users think of your site? Is it easy to use? What could be better?

Strategy

Your problem: quite a few of us produce content so the quality varies, our brand message gets diluted and we sometimes feel like we're playing catch-up

I'll produce a written report or a powerpoint presentation if you prefer. I might also run up some wireframes to show how content could be structured on key pages. Then we'll sit down over a cup of tea and develop a plan of action. I'll bring my suggestions to the table – and maybe some cake. You will too. Your content strategy has to be sustainable over the longer-term, which means working out who on your team will need to do what. And whether this is possible in the real world.

Implementation

Your problem: we want to improve our content strategy but we don't have the time or expertise to do it all in-house

Don't worry, you're nearly there! You'll start implementing your new, sustainable content strategy, but I'll still be there to help with training and questions. Congratulations! You now have content that is working for its keep. But what if you don't have the time or perhaps the in-house expertise to keep curating your content? In this case, I can offer a few companies a monthly content strategy service. I'll be posting more details soon, but for now get in touch and we can talk it through.
